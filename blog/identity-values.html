Hello everyone! I’m Rowan Strang. Today, I want to share some insights into my identity and values. (I really struggled with this task)

Disclosure... I answered the questions in sprint 1, in a series of poorly constructed simple answers. I've since fed those back through Chat-GPT for the following more redable blog post

Integrity and Honesty
Honesty is very important to me, even though it can sometimes put me in tough spots. For example, if I found a wallet with money, I might take the money but ensure the wallet gets to the police so the owner can recover it. This shows a practical side of me. I think about personal benefit but also try to do the right thing by helping the wallet’s owner get their belongings back. ultimatly though the amount of money in the wallet, the type of wallet and other judgements i make based on the wallet would inform my decision.

Compassion and Empathy
I’m the type of person who helps others when they ask for it, like helping someone at the supermarket. I may not go out of my way to find people to help, but I’m always ready to assist when someone needs me right there and then.

Responsibility and Procrastination
I’ll admit, I tend to put off tasks I don’t enjoy until the last minute. This approach allows me to work when I feel most ready, balancing my personal comfort with the need to get things done. It’s a practical way of handling responsibilities, especially when I’m not excited about the tasks at hand.

Fairness
I believe in fairness, but I’m also realistic about group dynamics. Sometimes, I’m okay with sharing credit with people who might not have contributed as much. Although it bothers me a bit, I compromise to keep things harmonious. This shows that while I value fairness, I’m also willing to bend a little to maintain peace.

Courage and Advocacy
I haven’t often been in situations where I needed to stand up for someone being treated unfairly. This might mean I’m either not in environments where this is common, or I’m cautious about getting involved. However, I’m always ready to support fairness and justice when the situation clearly calls for it.

Independence and Adaptability
I highly value my independence and ability to adapt to new situations. Being self-employed for over a decade has taught me how to manage myself and thrive under changing conditions. However, this same independence sometimes makes it challenging for me to stay focused, especially when I’m not passionate about the task at hand.

Generosity and Sustainability
I believe in generosity and think it’s important to consider the greater good over personal gain, particularly when it comes to sustainability. This belief guides my actions, from how I run my business to my everyday decisions. I try to balance my needs with the impact of my actions on the community and the environment.

Cultural Influence and Personal Growth Growing up, I was taught to respect all cultures and identities, which has become a natural part of who I am. However, my background and experiences have also made me aware of my biases, especially regarding gender, stemming from attending an all-boys school. Recognizing these biases has been crucial in my personal growth, helping me strive to be more understanding and inclusive.

Looking Ahead
As I continue my journey at Dev Academy, I’m excited to meet new people and dive deeper into the tech world, turning my passions into practical skills. This new chapter is not just about professional growth but also about personal development, as I navigate my values and identity in this changing landscape. I believe that understanding and articulating one’s values is a journey in itself, one that requires honesty, reflection, and a willingness to adapt and grow. I’m eager to see where this path takes me and how my values will evolve with the new experiences ahead.
